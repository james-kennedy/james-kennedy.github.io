Robots in Education

My PhD research focused on the use of social robots in the education of young children (aged 6-10). I believe that a robot with specialised social behaviour will improve the knowledge of a child at a greater rate than a robot without this social behaviour. I explored this hypothesis using the Nao robot and children from primary schools in the U.K. Please see my publications list to keep up-to-date with my most recent findings.

My research produced many surprising and unexpected results. I've found that robots can improve children's learning when compared to just a computer alone, but that there is a complex and intriguing relationship between the social behaviour of robots and learning. All of my experiments were performed in real-world environments, with almost completely autonomous robot behaviours.
