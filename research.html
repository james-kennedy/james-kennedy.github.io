Robots in Education

My research is focused on the use of social robots in the education of young children (aged 6-10). I believe that a robot with specialised social behaviour will improve the knowledge of a child at a greater rate than a robot without this social behaviour. I have already begun to explore this hypothesis using the Nao robot and children from primary schools local to Devon in the U.K. Please see my publications list to keep up-to-date with my most recent findings.

So far my research has continually turned out surprising and unexpected results. I've found that robots can improve children's learning when compared to just a computer alone, but that there is a complex and intriguing relationship between the social behaviour of robots and learning. This is incredibly exciting, as there are so many avenues to explore and I'm never quite sure what I'll be doing next! All of my experiments are performed in real-world environments: school classrooms - get in touch if you happen to be a teacher in Devon and want to get involved!
